About

I'm an academic leader and scholar-practitioner at Charles Sturt University, where I work as Academic Director, Education Strategy in the Division of Learning and Teaching. My work sits at the intersection of curriculum quality, programmatic assessment, academic governance, and the role of AI in reshaping what teaching and learning can look like.

Much of what drives me is a practical question: how do institutions move from good ideas to sustained change? I've spent the last several years leading initiatives that try to answer that — including the Models of Engagement and Assessment (MEA) framework, a university-wide transformation spanning 293 courses designed to reposition the course as the primary unit of curriculum and assessment design.

The MEA isn't about redesigning curriculum. It's about confirming what's already there — and making the architecture visible enough to build on.

Alongside institutional work, I'm an active scholar in the SoTL tradition. I write and speak about assessment reform, faculty identity in the age of AI, distributed leadership, and equity in regional higher education. I hold a Senior Fellowship with AdvanceHE and am currently pursuing Principal Fellowship.

My PhD is in Environmental Management and my MEd is in Learning and Leadership — a combination that has shaped a career at the intersection of systems thinking and educational practice.

Projects

Selected initiatives

Institutional work spanning curriculum, assessment, governance, and capability.

2024–2029 · Ongoing

Models of Engagement and Assessment (MEA)

Institution-wide transformation repositioning the course as the primary unit of curriculum and assessment design across 293 CSU courses. Four phases: Foundations, Mapping, Implementation, Assurance — aligned with HESF and TEQSA requirements.

2025–2027 · Ongoing

Faculty AI Response Plan

Strategic framework for AI-informed assessment reform and supervised assessment governance at CSU. Designed to strengthen institutional oversight while supporting staff to teach and assess with integrity in an AI-adjacent context.

2022 · Ongoing

Faculty Learning & Teaching Hub

Founded and led this professional learning platform, now with 8,000+ staff engagements. A core vehicle for building capability in inclusive curriculum design, authentic assessment, and academic integrity across the Faculty of Science and Health.

2019–2021

HEPPP Retention Project

Equity-focused retention initiative developing early-assessment strategies that improved outcomes in high-priority subjects. Trained 30+ staff in online invigilation protocols, enabling over 1,300 supervised exam sittings.

Research · Ongoing

Distributed leadership autoethnography

Collaborative autoethnographic research with senior CSU colleagues examining distributed leadership through the MEA transformation. Positioned for submission to journals in educational leadership and SoTL.

2022–2023

Brightspace LMS Transition

Led a faculty-wide platform transition to 100% readiness, including staff capability programs showcased to 150+ colleagues. A practical exercise in change management and technology adoption at scale.

PhD · 2009

A Passing Occupation

Doctoral research exploring the history and heritage of itinerant workers in rural New South Wales, 1850–1914 — recovering a hidden chapter of colonial life. View research →

Honours · 2004

Fire Management Planning & Cultural Heritage in NSW

Honours research examining whether mandatory Bush Fire Risk Management Plans across NSW adequately protect cultural heritage assets — finding systematic gaps in planning for both indigenous and historic heritage. View research →
