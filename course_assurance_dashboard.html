Reading guide

Understanding this Dashboard

This dashboard is a prototype governance tool for course-level assessment — sometimes called programmatic assessment internationally. It maps Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs) through Subject Learning Outcomes (SLOs) to individual assessments, and tracks how a student's evidence accumulates across a degree toward four developmental thresholds. It is designed for use by academic staff, course teams, and School Assessment Committees.

Key concepts
Course Learning Outcome (CLO)

The defined knowledge, skills, and capabilities a student must demonstrate to be awarded the degree. CLOs are set at course level, aligned with AQF Level 7 requirements, and do not change when individual subjects are updated. This course has 9 CLOs.

Subject Learning Outcome (SLO)

The specific capabilities assessed within a single subject. SLOs are the building blocks — a student demonstrating an SLO provides evidence toward one or more CLOs. The chain is: Assessment → SLO → CLO.

Developmental Thresholds

A shared language for how capability grows across a degree. There are four levels:

FoundationalIntroduced — Level 100 subjects
DevelopingBuilding — Level 200 subjects
IntegratedApplying across contexts — Level 300
MasteredConfirmed at capstone / graduation
Stage Boundaries

Formal checkpoints where CLO attainment is reviewed before a student progresses. Boundaries are triggered by academic level transition — not calendar year — so they work equitably for part-time and non-linear study patterns. At each boundary, all 9 CLOs must reach the required threshold.

192 Credit Point Structure

This AQF Level 7 degree is 192 credit points. Not all subjects are equal — students complete all Core subjects, choose one Compulsory Foundational set, then select Restricted Electives to reach 104 cp. This means different students may have different CLO coverage depending on which electives they choose.

CLO Mapping — how it works

In this prototype, CLO→SLO alignment is auto-computed by matching keywords between CLO descriptions and SLO text. A subject is considered to address a CLO if two or more keywords match. In production, these mappings should be confirmed by subject coordinators and ratified through course governance.

What each tab shows
Tab 1 Course Structure

A complete, navigable view of the degree. Subjects are organised by their structural role — Core (mandatory), Compulsory Foundational (choose one set), Restricted Electives (pool of 38, student selects 104 cp worth), and Unrestricted Elective.

How to use it: Click any subject row to expand it and see all its SLOs and assessments. Use the prefix filters in the Restricted Electives section to browse by discipline area. Each expanded subject also shows which CLOs it aligns to as coloured tags.

Governance use: Confirm that all subjects have SLOs and assessments recorded. Subjects showing "No assessments in system" cannot contribute threshold evidence and should be prioritised for data completion.
Tab 2 CLO Mapping

A matrix showing which subjects provide evidence toward each of the 9 CLOs. Each row is a subject; each CLO column shows a filled circle (✓) if the subject aligns to it, or an empty circle if not.

How to use it: Click a CLO card at the top to filter the table to only subjects aligned to that CLO. Use the Role filter (Core / Compulsory / Elective) to check whether CLO coverage is reliant on elective choices students may not make. Look for CLOs with sparse columns — these may be under-served by the current course design.

Important: Mappings in this prototype are auto-generated and must be verified by subject coordinators before use in formal governance decisions or accreditation submissions.
Tab 3 Student Progress

A longitudinal view of an individual student's CLO attainment. Shows credit point progress, four stage boundary checkpoints, a threshold track for each of the 9 CLOs, and a full submission history with CLO tags and threshold levels.

How to use it: Select a student from the dropdown. Read the stage boundary indicators first — these tell you whether the student is confirmed, in progress, or has evidence gaps at each checkpoint. Then examine the CLO threshold cards: the four coloured segments represent Foundational, Developing, Integrated, Mastered from left to right. Filled segments indicate accumulated evidence at that level.

Remediation alerts: An amber alert panel appears when a student is behind threshold for their current stage. It lists subjects in progress (complete remaining assessments) and available subjects not yet undertaken (enrolment conversation). A red course design gap flag means the gap is a curriculum issue, not a student issue.
Tab 4 Governance Overview

A course-level summary intended for Academic Board, School Assessment Committee, and accreditation reporting. Shows key metrics, CLO coverage across the subject pool, threshold distribution by stage, and assessment counts by subject role.

How to use it: The CLO coverage table shows what proportion of subjects address each CLO — thin bars signal potential risk. The threshold coverage matrix shows whether each CLO has subjects at each developmental level. Empty cells in the Integrated or Mastered columns are the most significant governance concern, as they mean students cannot accumulate evidence at that level for that CLO regardless of effort.

Governance use: Use this view as the basis for the Course Assurance Plan review at School Assessment Committee stage boundaries and Comprehensive Course Review cycles.
What this dashboard does not do
✗
Replace academic judgement
Threshold attainment is evidence-informed, not automatically determined. Academic staff confirm all progression decisions.
✗
Assign grades
The dashboard tracks evidence accumulation, not marks. GPA and award rules are entirely unchanged.
✗
Guarantee accreditation
It provides the evidentiary record. The judgement about whether accreditation standards are met is made by academic staff and committees.
✗
Replace the student record system
Student data in this prototype is simulated. Production deployment requires integration with enrolment and assessment systems.
✗
Confirm CLO mappings
Auto-generated mappings are a starting point only. Subject coordinators must verify them through course governance before use.
✗
Apply equally to all elective combinations
Because students choose different restricted electives, CLO coverage varies between students. The dashboard shows individual profiles, not a single universal course map.
Regulatory and policy context
HESF 2021
Higher Education Standards Framework. Domains 1, 3 and 5 require formal confirmation of staged CLO attainment. This dashboard provides the evidentiary infrastructure to support that requirement.
TEQSA
TEQSA guidance on generative AI emphasises coherent course-level assessment with triangulated longitudinal evidence. Detection-based responses to AI alone are insufficient — this model is the substantive response.
AQF Level 7
Bachelor degree — 192 credit points. The four developmental thresholds align with AQF descriptors for breadth and depth of knowledge, cognitive skills, and personal and professional skills at Level 7.
Glossary
CLOCourse Learning Outcome — the capability a student must demonstrate to be awarded the degree. Set at course level, aligned to AQF.
SLOSubject Learning Outcome — the specific capability assessed within one subject. Multiple SLOs contribute evidence toward CLOs.
Course Assurance PlanA whole-of-course blueprint identifying where each CLO is introduced, developed, integrated and confirmed across stages. This dashboard is its operational expression.
Developmental thresholdOne of four levels — Foundational, Developing, Integrated, Mastered — describing how capability grows across the degree.
Stage boundaryA formal checkpoint triggered by level transition (100→200, 200→300, 300→capstone) at which all CLOs are reviewed against the expected threshold.
Triangulated evidenceEvidence drawn from multiple assessments, contexts and assessors. Progression decisions should be based on patterns of evidence, not single tasks.
Remediation pathwayA structured opportunity for a student to provide additional evidence toward a CLO where threshold has not been met — without repeating an entire subject.
Course design gapA finding where no assessed subjects are mapped to a CLO at a required threshold level. Requires course team action — not a student issue.
CDAPCurriculum Design and Assurance Platform — the system from which the subject, SLO and assessment data in this dashboard is derived.
Programmatic assessmentThe international term for longitudinal, systems-based assessment design. At CSU referred to as course-level assessment to reflect local curriculum architecture.
